## Welcome!

FreeCAD is a community-driven, free and open-source project built by people with diverse skills, interests, and backgrounds.
Whether you are a developer, a designer, a technical writer, an experienced CAD professional, an hobbyist, or just curious, there are many ways to get involved, contribute and make an impact.

This Development Handbook guides through the FreeCAD ecosystem, setting up a development environment, the tools, and contribution processes.


## Where To Contribute

FreeCAD is a wide ecosystem:

- **Core development**: Improve features, fix bugs, or optimize performance in FreeCAD itself.
- **Addons**: Create or maintain external macros and workbenches that expand FreeCAD’s capabilities.
- **User Interface and User Experience**: Help refine workflows, usability, and design.
- **Documentation**: Write tutorials, improve guides, or translate content.
- **Website and Infrastructure**: Contribute to web platforms, CI, packaging, and tooling.
- **Community support**: Help users on forums, review issues, or triage bug reports.

No contribution is too small. Every improvement helps.


## Getting Involved

Joining the community is a great way to start:

- Introduce yourself and connect with other contributors on the [Forum](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FreeCAD/issues?q=is%3Aissue%20state%3Aopen%20label%3A%22Good%20first%20issue%22) or other social platforms.
- Follow ongoing discussions and development efforts.
- Start small: pick an issue, improve documentation, or test a feature.

Feel free to ask questions. Contributors are happy to help on-boarding newcomers.


## Finding Your Way

If you are not sure where to start:

- Look for [good first issues](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FreeCAD/issues?q=is%3Aissue%20state%3Aopen%20label%3A%22Good%20first%20issue%22) in the tracker.
- Explore areas that match your interests or skills.
- Check projects discussions, roadmaps, or team pages.

No need to understand everything. No previous coding experience is needed. Learning happens along the way.


## Help and Resources

Plenty of resources are available:

- Three main documentation portals
- Community forums and chat channels
- Issue trackers and code repositories

If stuck, ask away! Clear questions and curiosity are always welcome.

Happy contributing!
